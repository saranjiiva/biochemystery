Hanging vs Strangulation
A Forensic Differentiation

Definitions

Hanging: Constriction of neck by ligature where body weight acts as constricting force.

Strangulation: Constriction of neck by external force without suspension of body.

Mechanism of Death

  • Asphyxia
  • Cerebral ischemia
  • Vagal inhibition
  • Cervical spine injury (judicial hanging)

Medicolegal Importance

  • Differentiates suicide vs homicide
  • Guides autopsy findings
  • Assists crime scene reconstruction

Key Forensic Differences

Feature Hanging Strangulation
Ligature Mark Oblique, upward, incomplete Horizontal, transverse, complete
Level Above thyroid cartilage Below thyroid cartilage
Saliva Dribbling Present Absent
Face Pale Congested
Hyoid Fracture Rare Common
External Injuries Minimal Often present
Manner Usually Suicidal Usually Homicidal
