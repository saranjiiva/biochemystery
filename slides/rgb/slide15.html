DKA vs HHS – Clinical & Biochemical Comparison
Diabetic Ketoacidosis (DKA)

• Common in Type 1 DM
• Absolute Insulin Deficiency
• Glucose: 250–600 mg/dL
• Ketone Bodies Present
• Metabolic Acidosis (↓ pH)
• Kussmaul Breathing
• Rapid Onset (Hours)
Hyperosmolar Hyperglycemic State (HHS)

• Common in Type 2 DM
• Relative Insulin Deficiency
• Glucose: >600 mg/dL
• Minimal or No Ketosis
• No Significant Acidosis
• Severe Dehydration
• Gradual Onset (Days)
Key Difference: DKA = Ketosis + Acidosis | HHS = Extreme Hyperglycemia + Dehydration
