Hanging vs Strangulation
A Forensic Differentiation

Definition

Hanging: Constriction of neck by ligature where body weight acts as constricting force.

Strangulation: Constriction of neck by external force without suspension of body.

Key Differences

Feature Hanging Strangulation
Ligature Mark Oblique, upward, incomplete Horizontal, transverse, complete
Level of Mark Above thyroid cartilage Below thyroid cartilage
Saliva Dribbling Present (common) Usually absent
Face Pale Congested, cyanosed
Fracture of Hyoid Rare (except elderly) Common
External Injuries Minimal Often present
Manner of Death Usually suicidal Usually homicidal

Mechanism of Death

  • Asphyxia
  • Cerebral ischemia
  • Vagal inhibition
  • Fracture-dislocation of cervical vertebra (judicial hanging)

Medicolegal Importance

  • Helps differentiate suicide vs homicide
  • Guides crime scene investigation
  • Important in suspicious deaths
