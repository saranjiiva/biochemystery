Mercy Killing (Euthanasia)
International Law Comparison with Indian Legal Position

1. Definition

Euthanasia: Intentional termination of life to relieve suffering in terminal illness.

  • Active: Direct act causing death (illegal in India)
  • Passive: Withdrawal of life support (legal in India)
  • PAS: Physician provides means, patient self-administers

2. Methods Followed in India

  • Withdrawal of ventilator
  • Stopping artificial nutrition
  • DNR (Do Not Resuscitate)
  • Palliative sedation

3. Timeline – Mercy Killing Law in India

1860 – IPC Sections 302 & 306 criminalize homicide & abetment of suicide.
1994 – P. Rathinam Case: Attempt to recognize right to die.
1996 – Gian Kaur Case: Right to die not part of Article 21.
2011 – Aruna Shanbaug Case: Passive euthanasia allowed under strict guidelines.
2018 – Common Cause Case: Living Will legalized; Passive euthanasia recognized.
2023 – Supreme Court simplifies Living Will procedure.

6. Current Legal Position in India

  • Passive euthanasia legal (2018 SC judgment)
  • Living Will legally valid
  • Active euthanasia punishable under IPC 302
  • Abetment of suicide punishable under IPC 306
