Forensic Odontology
Role of Teeth in Medicolegal Identification

1. Definition

Forensic odontology is the application of dental science to legal investigations, primarily for identification of human remains and bite mark analysis.

2. Dental Anatomy (Forensic Importance)

  • Enamel – hardest tissue (resists fire & decomposition)
  • Pulp – rich source of DNA
  • Teeth survive extreme conditions

3. Age Estimation

  • Tooth eruption pattern (children)
  • Attrition & wear
  • Secondary dentin deposition
  • Cementum annulations
  • Gustafson’s method

4. DNA Analysis from Teeth

  • Pulp tissue extraction
  • Mitochondrial DNA analysis
  • Useful in burnt or decomposed bodies
  • Mass disaster identification

5. Bite Mark Analysis

  • Photographic documentation
  • Impression analysis
  • Inter-canine distance measurement
  • Comparison with dental casts

6. Applications in Identification

Application Use
Mass Disasters Comparison with dental records
Burn Victims Teeth resist high temperatures
Unknown Bodies Age & identity estimation
Child Abuse Bite mark evaluation

7. Forensic Workflow

Recovery of Remains
→
Dental Examination
→
Record Comparison
→
Identification

8. Medicolegal Importance

  • Reliable identification method
  • Highly durable biological evidence
  • Accepted in court testimony
  • Supports DNA and fingerprint analysis
