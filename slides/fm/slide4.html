Alcohol Intoxication & Drunken Driving
Forensic, Clinical & Medicolegal Perspective

Mechanism of Alcohol Intoxication

Primary Action: CNS Depressant

  • Enhances GABA activity → Sedation
  • Inhibits NMDA receptors → Cognitive impairment
  • Decreases cortical inhibition → Disinhibition
  • Depresses respiratory center (high levels)

Metabolism:

  • Liver (Alcohol Dehydrogenase)
  • Zero-order kinetics (~15 mg/dL/hour)

Clinical Stages (Based on BAC)

BAC (mg/dL) Clinical Features
20–50 Mild euphoria, impaired judgment
50–100 Poor coordination, delayed reaction
100–200 Ataxia, slurred speech
200–300 Confusion, stupor
>300 Coma, respiratory depression

Drunken Driving (Medicolegal Aspects)

Legal Blood Alcohol Limit:

  • Most countries: 0.05% – 0.08% (50–80 mg/dL)
  • Impairment begins below legal limit

Evidence Collection:

  • Breathalyzer test
  • Blood alcohol estimation
  • Urine sample
  • Clinical examination

Common Driving Impairments:

  • Delayed reaction time
  • Poor judgment
  • Visual impairment
  • Risk-taking behavior

Autopsy Findings in Alcohol-Related Death

External:
  • Odor of alcohol
  • Injuries from road traffic accident
Internal:
  • Congested organs
  • Fatty liver (chronic cases)
  • Gastric mucosal congestion
  • Pulmonary edema
Cause of Death:
  • Respiratory depression
  • Head injury (RTA)
  • Aspiration of vomitus
