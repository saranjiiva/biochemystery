Organophosphorus Poisoning
Exposure → Mechanism → Clinical Features → Complications → Autopsy

Sources & Exposure

  • Agricultural pesticides (Parathion, Malathion)
  • Ingestion (suicidal common)
  • Inhalation (spraying)
  • Dermal absorption

Mechanism of Toxicity

  • Irreversible inhibition of Acetylcholinesterase (AChE)
  • Accumulation of Acetylcholine
  • Continuous stimulation of:
    • Muscarinic receptors
    • Nicotinic receptors
    • CNS receptors

Aging Phenomenon

  • Loss of alkyl group from OP-AChE complex
  • Permanent enzyme inactivation
  • Oximes ineffective after aging

Clinical Features

Muscarinic (SLUDGE):
  • Salivation
  • Lacrimation
  • Urination
  • Diarrhea
  • GI cramps
  • Emesis
DUMBELS:
  • Diarrhea
  • Urination
  • Miosis
  • Bronchospasm
  • Emesis
  • Lacrimation
  • Salivation
Nicotinic:
  • Muscle fasciculation
  • Weakness
  • Respiratory paralysis
CNS:
  • Confusion
  • Convulsions
  • Coma

Complications

  • Intermediate Syndrome (24–96 hrs)
  • Delayed Polyneuropathy (1–3 weeks)

Investigations

  • Plasma cholinesterase ↓
  • RBC AChE ↓ (more specific)
  • ABG – Respiratory acidosis
  • Toxicology screening

Treatment

DrugAction
AtropineAntimuscarinic
PralidoximeReactivates AChE
DiazepamControls seizures
VentilationPrevents hypoxia

Autopsy Findings

External:
  • Froth at mouth & nostrils
  • Miosis
  • Characteristic odor
Internal:
  • Pulmonary edema
  • Congested brain
  • Fluid blood
  • Gastric mucosal congestion
Histopathology:
  • Alveolar edema
  • Neuronal hypoxia

Forensic Sample Preservation

  • Stomach contents
  • Blood (sodium fluoride)
  • Liver, kidney
  • Preserved in saturated saline
