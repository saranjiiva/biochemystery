Toxins & System Blocks

External Agents Disrupting TCA Flux

Heavy Metal

Arsenite

Direct Enzyme Inhibitor

Mechanism

Binds irreversibly to sulfhydryl (-SH) groups of Lipoic Acid, disabling the E2 subunits.

Affected Enzymes

  • PDH Complex: Pyruvate accumulation.
  • α-Ketoglutarate DH: Cycle block at step 4.
ETC Blocker

Cyanide

Respiratory Chain Arrest

Mechanism

Inhibits Complex IV (Cytochrome c oxidase) in ETC. Prevents O2 reduction.

Effect on TCA

  • Massive accumulation of NADH & FADH2.
  • Lack of oxidized NAD+/FAD stops TCA dehydrogenases via product inhibition.
Metabolic

Ethanol Excess

Redox State Disruption

Mechanism

Ethanol metabolism generates massive amounts of NADH in the liver (High NADH/NAD+ ratio).

Consequences

  • Inhibits Isocitrate DH & α-KGDH.
  • Shunts Acetyl-CoA to Ketogenesis (Ketoacidosis) instead of TCA.

Clinical Insight: The TCA cycle requires both functional enzymes (blocked by Arsenite) and a supply of oxidized cofactors from the ETC (blocked by Cyanide/Hypoxia).
