Pyruvate Carboxylase

Primary Anaplerotic Reaction (Replenishing TCA)
Cofactor Required

Biotin (Vitamin B7)

Reaction: Carboxylation

Pyruvate 3 Carbon
+ ATP + CO₂ Irreversible
Oxaloacetate 4 Carbon

Regulation: Obligately activated by Acetyl-CoA. High Acetyl-CoA signals need for OAA.

1. Biotin Carboxylation

ATP-dependent formation of N-carboxybiotin intermediate.

2. Swinging Arm Transfer

Biotin arm swings the activated CO₂ from one active site to the other.

3. Transcarboxylation

CO₂ is transferred to pyruvate to form Oxaloacetate.

Oxaloacetate (OAA)

The 4-carbon product essential for:

  • Entering TCA cycle (combining with Acetyl-CoA)
  • Gluconeogenesis precursor

Biotin Mechanism

Acts as a CO₂ carrier attached to a lysine residue (prosthetic group).

Clinical Relevance: Multiple Carboxylase Deficiency (biotin metabolic defect) or excessive raw egg white consumption (Avidin) prevents this step, causing lactic acidosis and alopecia.
