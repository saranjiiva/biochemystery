Pyruvate Carboxylase

Primary Anaplerotic Reaction (Replenishes TCA)
Cofactor Required

Biotin (Vitamin B7)

Overall Reaction

Pyruvate + ATP + CO₂ → Oxaloacetate

Regulation: Obligately activated by Acetyl-CoA → signals need for OAA.

1. Biotin Carboxylation

ATP activates bicarbonate → CO₂ binds to biotin forming N-carboxybiotin.

2. Swinging Arm Mechanism

Biotin covalently attached to lysine swings between two active sites.

3. Transcarboxylation

CO₂ transferred from biotin to pyruvate → forms oxaloacetate.

Oxaloacetate (OAA)

  • Condenses with Acetyl-CoA → citrate
  • Key substrate for gluconeogenesis
Biotin
CO₂

Biotin Function

Prosthetic group bound to lysine → acts as a mobile CO₂ carrier.

Clinical Relevance: Biotin deficiency (raw egg white → avidin) or multiple carboxylase deficiency → lactic acidosis, alopecia, dermatitis.
