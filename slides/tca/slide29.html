TCA Cycle Inhibitors

Specific Enzyme Blockades & Mechanisms

Competitive

Malonate

Inhibits Succinate Dehydrogenase (Complex II)

Mechanism

Structural analog of Succinate. It binds to the active site of SDH but cannot undergo dehydrogenation (lacks the CH2-CH2 bond needed for oxidation to fumarate).

Kinetic Impact

  • Competes with Succinate: Can be overcome by increasing substrate concentration.
  • Km Increases: Lower affinity for substrate.
  • Vmax Unchanged: Enzyme capacity remains same.
Suicide Substrate

Fluoroacetate

Inhibits Aconitase ("Trojan Horse")

Mechanism

Not toxic itself. Converted to Fluoroacetyl-CoA, then condensed with Oxaloacetate to form Fluorocitrate. This analog binds irreversibly to Aconitase, halting the cycle.

Clinical/Tox Relevance

  • Rat Poison (1080): Historically used as a rodenticide.
  • Metabolic Block: Causes massive accumulation of Citrate.
  • Result: Energy failure and death (no antidote, supportive care only).

Note: Inhibitors help elucidate metabolic pathways. Malonate was key for Krebs to deduce the cyclic nature of the pathway (succinate accumulation).
