Anaplerotic Reactions

Replenishing TCA Cycle Intermediates

Major

Pyruvate Carboxylase

Pyruvate → Oxaloacetate

Significance

Most critical anaplerotic reaction in liver and kidney. Maintains OAA levels for Gluconeogenesis and TCA capacity.

Requirements

  • Cofactor: Biotin (Vitamin B7)
  • Activator: Acetyl-CoA (obligate)
  • Energy: Requires 1 ATP
Protein

Transaminations

Glutamate ↔ α-Ketoglutarate

Mechanism

Reversible reactions using PLP (B6) to convert amino acids into TCA intermediates (α-KG, OAA).

Key Reactions

  • GDH: Glutamate → α-KG + NH4+
  • Aspartate: AST converts Asp → OAA
  • Links protein catabolism to energy
Lipid/AA

Succinyl-CoA Entry

Val, Ile, Met, Odd-Chain FAs

Pathway

Propionyl-CoA → Methylmalonyl-CoA → Succinyl-CoA. Enters cycle at 4-carbon stage.

Clinical Note

  • Vit B12: Required for mutase step.
  • Deficiency: Methylmalonic Acidemia.
  • Replenishes Succinyl-CoA pool.

Definition: "Anaplerotic" (Greek: to fill up) reactions replenish intermediates drained for biosynthesis (cataplerosis), preventing cycle collapse.
