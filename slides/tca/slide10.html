Clinical Correlations

Pathology of the PDH Complex

Cofactor

Thiamine (B1) Deficiency

Affects E1 (TPP)

Mechanism

Lack of TPP impairs PDH & α-KGDH. Aerobic tissues (brain/heart) fail to produce ATP.

Clinical Presentations

  • Beriberi: Dry (neuro) or Wet (cardiac failure).
  • Wernicke-Korsakoff: Ataxia, ophthalmoplegia, memory loss (common in alcoholics).
Genetic

PDH Complex Deficiency

Mostly X-linked (E1α)

Mechanism

Pyruvate cannot enter TCA cycle. Shunted to Lactate via LDH. Causes severe lactic acidosis.

Management

  • Ketogenic Diet: High fat, low carb.
  • Generates Acetyl-CoA from fatty acids (bypassing PDH) and ketone bodies for brain fuel.
Toxin

Arsenic Poisoning

Inhibits Lipoic Acid (E2)

Mechanism

Arsenite binds to thiol (-SH) groups of lipoic acid, locking E2 in inactive state. Stops PDH & α-KGDH.

Clinical Features

  • Vomiting, rice-water stools, garlic breath.
  • QT prolongation.
  • Similar energy deficit to thiamine deficiency but with GI toxicity.

Common Theme: Any disruption of the PDH complex prevents aerobic glucose oxidation, leading to energy failure (ATP↓) and Lactic Acidosis.
