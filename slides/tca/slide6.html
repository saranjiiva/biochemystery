E2: Dihydrolipoyl Transacetylase

Enzyme Component 2 of PDH Complex (Core)
Cofactors Required

Lipoate & Coenzyme A

Reaction: Acetyl Transfer

Input Hydroxyethyl-TPP + CoA-SH
Output Acetyl-CoA + Reduced Lipoamide

"Generates the high-energy thioester bond"

1. Swinging Arm Pickup

Lipoamide (attached to Lysine) swings to E1, accepts acetyl group, and is reduced.

2. Transthiolesterification

Acetyl group is transferred from lipoamide sulfur to CoA sulfur.

3. Energy Conservation

Oxidation energy is preserved in the thioester bond of Acetyl-CoA.

Focus: Lipoamide & CoA

Lipoic Acid: Internal disulfide bridge

CoA: Panthothenic acid derivative

Clinical Correlation: Arsenite (Arsenic poisoning) binds irreversibly to the dithiol groups of reduced lipoamide, completely inhibiting the PDH complex.
