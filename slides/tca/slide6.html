E2: Dihydrolipoyl Transacetylase

Second enzyme of the Pyruvate Dehydrogenase Complex (Core enzyme)

Reaction

Hydroxyethyl-TPP + CoA → Acetyl-CoA + Reduced lipoamide
  • E2 transfers the acetyl group to Coenzyme A.
  • Forms the high-energy thioester bond of acetyl-CoA.
  • Contains a flexible lipoamide swinging arm.
Cofactors: Lipoic acid (lipoamide) and Coenzyme A (Vitamin B₅ derivative)

Mechanism (Steps)

  • Acetyl transfer: Acetyl group moves from hydroxyethyl-TPP to oxidized lipoamide.
  • Reduction: Lipoamide disulfide bond is reduced.
  • Transthiolesterification: Acetyl group is transferred from lipoamide to CoA.
  • Product formed: Acetyl-CoA enters the TCA cycle.
Lipoamide swinging arm transfers acetyl group
Clinical correlation: Arsenite poisoning binds to reduced lipoamide and inhibits PDH complex, leading to lactic acidosis.
