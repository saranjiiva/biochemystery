E3: Dihydrolipoyl Dehydrogenase

Third enzyme of the Pyruvate Dehydrogenase Complex
Reduced lipoamide → NADH + H⁺
  • E3 catalyzes reoxidation of reduced lipoamide.
  • Contains tightly bound FAD and uses NAD⁺ as final electron acceptor.
  • Electrons flow: Lipoamide → FAD → NAD⁺.
  • Generates NADH for oxidative phosphorylation.
Clinical relevance: E3 (DLD) deficiency affects PDH, α-KGDH, and BCKDH complexes → lactic acidosis & neurological dysfunction.

Cofactors & Electron Flow

  • FAD = Vitamin B₂ derivative (enzyme-bound)
  • NAD⁺ = Vitamin B₃ derivative (mobile carrier)
  • Restores oxidized lipoamide for next PDH cycle
