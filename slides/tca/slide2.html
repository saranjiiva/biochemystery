History of the TCA Cycle

Discovery and development of the citric acid cycle
1930s
Szent-Györgyi
Discovered organic acids (succinate, fumarate, malate) involved in cellular oxidation.
1937
Hans Krebs
Proposed the cyclic nature of the citric acid pathway (Krebs cycle).
1950s
PDH Complex
Structure and function of pyruvate dehydrogenase complex elucidated.
1960s
Lipoamide Arm
Swinging arm mechanism explaining substrate channeling discovered.
1970s
Chemiosmosis
Mitchell linked NADH production to ATP synthesis.
